# KidsHub — Sales Demo Guide

Everything you need to walk a daycare owner from "What's this?" → "I want
to try it." Target length: **20 minutes**, optimized for in-person tablet
demo but works just as well on a shared screen.

---

## Pre-call checklist (5 min the morning of)

- [ ] Log into the demo tenant in the **owner dashboard** (`demo@getkidshub.com`).
- [ ] Log a second browser profile into the **kidshub parent app** as a
      demo parent in the same daycare.
- [ ] Log a third browser profile into the **teacher app** as a demo teacher.
- [ ] Confirm the demo daycare has **Pro** enabled in `config/product.ts`
      (or flip `demoMode: true` to unlock everything).
- [ ] Queue tabs in this order: **Dashboard → Children → Classrooms →
      Staff → Messages → Schedule → Plans**.
- [ ] Have the parent app already on the **Home** tab.
- [ ] Have the teacher app on the **Classroom** tab.
- [ ] Open `PRODUCT_PLAN.md` in a secret tab in case they ask about a
      feature that isn't done yet — be honest, point to the roadmap.

## The 20-minute flow

### 1. Framing (2 min)

> "I know you're busy. I'm going to show you three things: the parent
> experience, the teacher experience, and the owner experience. By the
> end you'll either want to pilot this, or you won't — either way I'm
> not going to waste your time."

**Ask, don't pitch:**
1. How many kids are you at today?
2. What do you currently use to talk to parents?
3. Biggest parent complaint?

Write their answers down. You'll reference them in the close.

### 2. Parent experience (5 min)

- Open the **parent app** on your phone (or mirrored on the tablet).
- **Home**: daily summary ("Ellie ate most of lunch, napped 2h, 3
  moments of joy"). Point out this is what the parent sees *automatically*
  — the teacher doesn't have to write a novel.
- **Photos**: tap into the gallery. "Every photo the teacher takes today
  shows up here grouped by day." Mention: *Pro feature*.
- **Messages**: two-way chat with the teacher. "No more lost papers, no
  texting personal cell numbers."
- **Schedule**: today's plan. Parent sees what their kid will do today.

**Key line:** *"Every single thing you see here replaces something you
currently do by text, paper, or word-of-mouth at pickup."*

### 3. Teacher experience (5 min)

- Switch to the **teacher app**.
- **Classroom** tab: "Here's the roster. Tap a child to log
  anything — food, nap, activity, photo, incident."
- Quick log one item live ("Ellie ate lunch, mostly finished").
- Show it pop up in the **parent** app in real time. This is the moment
  that sells it.
- Walk through the **Photos** upload flow on the teacher app.
- Show **Health log** (Pro): "If anything health-related happens, it's
  one tap — parent gets notified, you get a record."

**Key line:** *"Your teachers are not going to fight this. It's faster
than a paper sheet, and kids get tagged once instead of written three
times."*

### 4. Owner dashboard (5 min)

- Switch to the **owner dashboard**.
- **Dashboard**: attendance today, messages, announcements — one
  screen.
- **Children** → open a profile. Show the full timeline: every log,
  photo, report.
- **Staff**: invite flow. "New teacher gets a link, clicks, logs in —
  that's it."
- **Schedule / Plans**: weekly planner — teachers fill this in, parents
  see it.
- **Announcements**: blast to all parents in one tap.
- **Plans page**: show them Starter → Pro → Premium pricing.

**Key line:** *"You don't need to learn a new tool — your teachers log,
and the dashboard fills itself in."*

### 5. Close (3 min)

Pick **one** of:

**A. Pilot close (preferred):**
> "Here's what I'd like to do: let's put your classroom on this for 60
> days, free — that's our Starter window. I'll set it up for you; you
> don't do anything. Before day 60 we either upgrade you to Pro or the
> account quietly locks — no surprise charge, no auto-billing. Sound
> fair?"

**B. Referral close:**
> "Even if this isn't for you, who do you know running a daycare that
> would want to see this? I'd love an intro."

**C. Price close (if they're already nodding):**
> "Pro is $X per child per month. With your enrollment that's about $Y
> a month. I can get you set up today."

## Common objections + the one-line answer

- **"My teachers won't use it."**
  → "Let's pilot with one classroom. You're on Starter free for 60
  days — if the teacher doesn't love it by then, you never paid
  anything and the account just locks. Zero risk."

- **"I already use [Brightwheel / Procare / Lillio]."**
  → "Great — they're solid. What's one thing you wish they did that
  they don't? [Listen.] KidsHub was built by a daycare parent who was
  frustrated by that exact thing."

- **"It's too expensive."**
  → "How much time do you spend on parent texts a week? [Their answer
  × their hourly rate] is what KidsHub saves you. Plus the parent
  experience brings referrals — the first 2 referrals pay for a year."

- **"What about parent privacy / compliance?"**
  → "Every photo stays inside your daycare. Parents only see their own
  child. Nothing is sold, nothing leaves your tenant. Happy to send
  you the data handling doc."

- **"Can I export my data if I leave?"**
  → "Yes — full CSV export of everything, any time, no penalty."

## What "good" looks like after the demo

- They schedule the setup call before you leave → **pilot**.
- They ask for an email summary → warm lead, send same day.
- They go quiet for 2+ weeks → cold, move on.

Keep your personal scoreboard honest. If you're doing <1 pilot per
5 demos, rework the **framing** — usually the pitch is selling
*features* instead of the *pickup-line problem*.
